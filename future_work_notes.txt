FUTURE WORK — DDE (Directional Distance Encoding)

The original SubgraphRAG paper uses a lightweight MLP with 
Directional Distance Encoding (DDE) for triple scoring.

DDE encodes structural distances from topic entities by 
propagating one-hot encodings bidirectionally through the graph.
This allows the retriever to score triples based on both 
semantic similarity AND structural proximity to the query entity.

In our implementation, we used cosine similarity for triple 
scoring (simpler but effective). A future improvement would be 
to implement DDE-based triple scoring using the MLP approach 
from the paper, which would likely improve multi-hop retrieval 
performance further.
